Lesson 2 Grammar: Introduction to “mga” , “ nga”, and “ka” and more Affixes

Three essential elements of Cebuano grammar: the plural marker “mga”, the linker “nga”, and the countable marker “ka”. These components are critical for constructing grammatically correct sentences in Cebuano.

numeral + ka + noun

isa lamesa

(one) (table)

1. The Countable Marker “ka”

The particle “ka” is used to link a numeral to a noun, indicating the quantity of the noun. It acts as a syntactic bridge, ensuring grammatical agreement between the numeral and the noun. Without “ka”, the phrase would feel incomplete or unnatural in Cebuano.

This particle is not to be confused with the short version pronoun of “Ikaw” which is also “ka”

2. The Linker “nga”

The particle “nga” is a versatile linker that connects adjectives to nouns, ensuring proper grammatical flow. It is also used in other contexts, such as linking relative clauses or possessives, but this guide focuses on its role with adjectives.

adjective + nga + noun

itom nga lamesa

(black) (table)

3. The Plural Marker “mga”

The particle “mga” indicates plurality, marking a noun as plural. It is used when referring to more than one of a noun, whether the exact number is specified or not.

mga + noun

mga lamesa

(tables)

Combining the Elements

In some cases, these particles can appear together in a sentence, following specific rules:

  • When a numeral and adjective are both used, the structure is:

numeral + ka + adjective + nga + noun

tulo ka itom nga lamesa

(three black tables)

Mga is not used with numerals but can combine with nga for to link plural nouns to an adjective

mga puti nga lingkuranan

Mastering mga, nga, and ka is essential for fluency in Cebuano. The countable marker ka links numerals to nouns, the linker nga connects adjectives to nouns, and the plural marker mga indicates multiple nouns. Through practice and the interactive game provided, you can develop a strong understanding of these fundamental particles.

 

Affixes Guide

  • i-, pag-, -i, and -a. These affixes are essential for modifying verb roots to indicate focus, aspect, or purpose in Cebuano sentences
  • The prefix i- is used to indicate that the verb focuses on the object of the action, often marking the noun that is directly affected or moved by the action. It is commonly used in object-focus constructions, where the object is the primary focus of the sentence. (e.g. i-butang)
  • The -i suffix often appears in imperative sentences, directing the action toward an object or location. It can also indicate a beneficiary, e.g., Sulati ang papel. (Write on the paper for someone.)
  • The -a suffix is often used in commands, focusing on the object affected by the action. It is distinct from -i in that it emphasizes direct action on the object rather than a beneficiary or location.
  • The prefix pag- indicates a general action or process, often focusing on the actor performing the action. It is used in actor-focus constructions and can denote habitual or ongoing actions. It is versatile and appears in various tenses. Pag- emphasizes the action itself or the actor performing it.
