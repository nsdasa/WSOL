## Getting Started

Welcome to the Bob and Mariel Ward School of Filipino Languages learning app! This web-based tool helps you learn Cebuano, Maranao, and Sinama with English translations.

**To start learning:**
1. Open your web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge work best)
2. Go to the website address provided by your teacher
3. Pick a language and lesson using the dropdowns at the top of the page
4. Click on any tab to start learning

---

## Main Navigation

At the top of the screen, you will see a row of tabs. These are your learning modules:

| Tab | What It Does |
|-----|--------------|
| **Flashcards** | View and study vocabulary words with pictures |
| **Match** | Match pictures to words in a fun game |
| **Audio Match** | Listen to words and match them to pictures |
| **Unsa Ni?** | Take a multiple-choice quiz |
| **Sentence** | Build sentences by arranging words |
| **Sentence Rev.** | Review sentence structures with pictures |
| **Grammar** | Read grammar lesson content |
| **Teacher's Guide** | View the teacher's guide for each lesson |
| **Print PDFs** | Create printable flashcards and worksheets |

**Note:** The "Deck Builder" and "Admin" tabs only appear after you log in.

## Selecting a Language and Lesson

Before you start any activity, choose what you want to study:

### How to Select:
1. **Language Dropdown** - Click the dropdown menu labeled "Language" at the top-left
2. Pick from:
   - Cebuano (ceb)
   - Maranao (mrw)
   - Sinama (sin)
3. **Lesson Dropdown** - Click the dropdown menu labeled "Lesson"
4. Pick the lesson number you want to study

The page will refresh automatically with the words for your selected language and lesson.

## Flashcards

Flashcards are the main way to learn new vocabulary.

### How to Use Flashcards:

1. **Click on any card** to flip it over
   - The front shows a picture
   - The back shows the word in your chosen language and English

2. **Play Audio** - Click the speaker icon (sound button) on the back of the card to hear how the word is pronounced

3. **Navigate Pages** - If your lesson has many words, use the page buttons at the bottom:
   - Click the arrow buttons to go forward or back
   - Click a page number to jump to that page

4. **Cards Per Page** - You can change how many cards show at once using the dropdown near the page controls

### What's on Each Card:

**Front (Picture Side):**
- A picture showing the meaning of the word
- The lesson number in the corner

**Back (Word Side):**
- The word in the language you're learning
- The English translation
- A sound button to hear the pronunciation
- Any notes or tips about the word (if available)

## Picture Match

This is a matching game where you connect pictures to words.

### How to Play:

1. **Choose Your Mode:**
   - **Review Mode** - No scoring, just practice. Wrong answers are shown immediately.
   - **Test Mode** - Your score is tracked. Try to get the best score!

2. **Click "Start"** to begin

3. **Match the Cards:**
   - Click a picture, then click the matching word
   - Or click a word, then click the matching picture
   - Correct matches will stay connected
   - Wrong matches will shake and reset

4. **Complete the Round** - Match all pairs to finish

5. **View Results** - See your score and accuracy at the end (in Test Mode)

### Tips:
- In Review Mode, you can take your time and learn
- In Test Mode, try to make fewer mistakes for a better score
- Pictures appear on the left, words appear on the right

---

## Audio Match

Similar to Picture Match, but you match sounds to pictures instead of text.

### How to Play:

1. **Choose Your Mode:**
   - **Review Mode** - Practice with no scoring
   - **Test Mode** - Get scored on your performance

2. **Click "Start"** to begin

3. **Listen and Match:**
   - Click a sound button to hear a word
   - Click the picture that matches what you heard
   - Correct matches stay connected
   - Wrong matches shake and reset

4. **Complete the Round** - Match all sounds to pictures to finish

### Tips:
- Click the sound button as many times as you need
- Listen carefully to the pronunciation
- This is great for training your ear to recognize spoken words

## Unsa Ni? Quiz

"Unsa Ni?" means "What is this?" in Cebuano. This is a multiple-choice quiz.

### How to Play:

1. **Choose Your Mode:**
   - **Review Mode** - Practice without scoring
   - **Test Mode** - Your answers are scored

2. **Click "Start"** to begin

3. **Answer Questions:**
   - You will see a picture
   - Choose the correct word from 4 options
   - Click your answer

4. **Get Feedback:**
   - In Review Mode, you see right away if you're correct
   - In Test Mode, your results are shown at the end

5. **Navigate Questions** - Use the arrows or number buttons to move between questions

### Tips:
- Read all the answer options before choosing
- In Test Mode, you cannot change your answer after clicking
- Try Review Mode first to learn, then Test Mode to check yourself

## Sentence Builder

Build sentences by arranging word cards in the correct order.

### How to Use:

1. **Select a Frame** - Sentence frames are shown as templates with blanks
   - Example: "_____ ang _____" (The _____ is _____)

2. **View Available Words** - Word cards are shown below the frame

3. **Drag and Drop:**
   - Click and drag a word card
   - Drop it into the correct blank space
   - Words snap into place if correct

4. **Check Your Work** - Some words fit multiple positions, so think about meaning

5. **Move Between Frames** - Use the navigation to try different sentence structures

### Tips:
- Look at the word types (verbs, nouns, adjectives) to help you place them
- The frame shows you the sentence pattern you're building
- Pictures on the cards help you understand what each word means

## Sentence Review

Review complete sentences with pictures representing each word.

### How to Use:

1. **Sentence Type Banner** - At the top, you'll see a colored banner showing the sentence type:
   - **Gold/Amber** - Question (with ? icon)
   - **Green** - Statement (with comment icon)
   - **Orange** - Command (with bullhorn icon)
   - **Blue** - Answer (with reply icon)

2. **View Sentences** - Each sentence is shown as a row of pictures
   - Each picture represents one word or phrase in the sentence
   - Click on pictures to flip them and see the word
   - Click the speaker icon to hear pronunciation

3. **Show Sentence Text** - Click the "Show Sentence Text" button to reveal:
   - **Word bubbles** appear below the picture cards
   - **Connection lines** draw from each picture down to its corresponding word bubble(s)
   - **Multiple words** from the same card (like "Maayong Adlaw") show separate bubbles with lines going to the same picture
   - **Function words** (like "mga") without pictures still show bubbles with connecting lines to their placeholder cards
   - **English translation** appears at the bottom

4. **Audio on Bubbles** - Click any word bubble with a speaker icon to hear that word pronounced

5. **Navigate Sentences** - Use Previous/Next buttons or arrow keys to move between sentences

6. **Sequences** - Sentences are grouped into sequences that tell a story. Click the sequence title to jump to different sequences.

### Tips:
- The connection lines help you see exactly which words belong to which picture
- This is great for understanding how phrase cards represent multiple words
- Say the sentence out loud as you look at each word bubble
- Great for reviewing after using the Sentence Builder

## Voice Practice

Practice your pronunciation by recording yourself and comparing to native speakers.

### How to Use:

1. **Enable Voice Practice** - Toggle the setting in your preferences

2. **Click the Microphone** - On any flashcard, click the voice practice button

3. **Listen First** - The native speaker's pronunciation will play automatically

4. **Record Yourself:**
   - Click "Start Practice"
   - A countdown will begin
   - Speak the word clearly when prompted

5. **Get Your Score:**
   - The app compares your pronunciation to the native speaker
   - You get a score from 0-100
   - Feedback tells you how to improve

### Score Breakdown:
- **Vowel Quality** - How well you pronounced the vowels
- **Pitch/Intonation** - Your tone and rhythm
- **Speech Timing** - How your speed matched the native speaker

### Tips:
- Use headphones for best results
- Record in a quiet place
- Try multiple times to improve your score
- A score above 70 is good, above 85 is excellent

## Deck Builder

The Deck Builder lets you create, edit, and manage flashcard content.

**Note:** You must be logged in to access this feature.

### Getting to Deck Builder:
1. Log in with an authorized account
2. Click the "Deck Builder" tab that appears

### Understanding the Interface:

**Summary Stats:**
- Total Cards - How many cards exist
- Complete - Cards with all assets (picture + audio)
- Missing Assets - Cards that need pictures or audio
- New Words - Recently added cards

**Card Data Table:**
Shows all cards with columns for:
- Card Number
- Word (in the selected language)
- English Translation
- Categories
- Picture status
- Audio status
- Overall status

### Basic Card Editing:

1. **Find a Card** - Scroll or use search to find it

2. **Click to Edit** - Click on any field to edit it:
   - Word spelling
   - English translation
   - Categories

3. **Save Changes** - Click "Save Changes" at the top when done

### Adding New Cards:

1. **Click "Add New Card"** - Button at top or bottom of table

2. **Fill In Information:**
   - Word in the language
   - English translation
   - Choose categories

3. **Save** - Click "Save Changes" to add the card

### Deleting Cards:

1. **Find the Card** - Use search or scroll

2. **Click Delete** - Click the trash icon on that row

3. **Confirm** - Confirm you want to delete

4. **Save** - Click "Save Changes"

### Recording Audio:

1. **Find the Card** - Locate the card needing audio

2. **Click Record** - Click the microphone icon in the Audio column

3. **Allow Microphone** - Give permission when asked

4. **Record:**
   - Wait for the countdown
   - Speak the word clearly
   - Recording stops automatically after silence

5. **Edit Your Recording:**
   - See the waveform (sound wave picture)
   - Drag the markers to trim the start/end
   - Click "Play Selection" to preview

6. **Save Audio** - Click "Save Audio" to upload

### Uploading Existing Audio:

1. **Find the Card** - Locate the card

2. **Click Upload** - Click the upload icon in the Audio column

3. **Choose File** - Select an MP3 or M4A file from your computer

4. **Confirm** - The file will upload and attach to the card

### For Deck Managers Only:

**CSV Data Management:**
- Upload Word List CSV files to bulk-update cards
- Export cards to CSV for backup or editing

**Media Files Upload:**
- Upload multiple pictures at once
- Upload multiple audio files at once
- Files must follow naming conventions

**Sentence Builder Data:**
- Upload sentence word lists
- Edit which words appear in sentence building exercises

**Sentence Review Data:**
- Create and edit sentence review lessons
- Import sentences from text or CSV

### Tips:
- Always click "Save Changes" before leaving
- Use filters to find specific cards quickly
- Back up your data regularly by exporting to CSV

## Troubleshooting

### Common Problems and Solutions:

**Problem: Page won't load**
- Check your internet connection
- Try refreshing the page (press F5 or click refresh)
- Clear your browser cache

**Problem: Audio won't play**
- Check that your sound is not muted
- Try a different browser
- Make sure the card has audio (look for the speaker icon)

**Problem: Can't record audio**
- Make sure you allowed microphone permission
- Check that your microphone is working
- Try a different browser (Chrome works best)

**Problem: Cards aren't showing**
- Make sure you selected a language and lesson
- Try clearing your filters
- Refresh the page

**Problem: Can't log in**
- Check that you selected your username correctly
- Make sure you typed the password correctly
- Ask your administrator if the account is active

**Problem: Changes won't save**
- Make sure you're logged in
- Click the "Save Changes" button
- Check for error messages at the top of the screen

**Problem: PDF won't generate**
- Make sure you have cards that match your filter
- Wait for the loading to complete
- Try a smaller lesson range if the file is too big

### Getting Help:

If you can't solve a problem:
1. Take a screenshot of the error
2. Note what you were trying to do
3. Contact your teacher or administrator
